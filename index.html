What "eco-conscious" means here

A garden is a community, not a decoration.

Every choice leans toward plants that belong here — the ones that feed insects, shelter birds, hold water, and build soil. The result is a landscape that looks alive because it is, and that asks less of you with each passing year.

Native-first

Rooted in this place

Designs built on Southeastern natives — serviceberry, oakleaf hydrangea, little bluestem, coneflower — plants adapted to our clay, heat, and rainfall.

For wildlife

Habitat that works

Keystone plants like native oaks and goldenrod feed the caterpillars and pollinators that songbirds depend on. A yard becomes part of a corridor.

For people

Made to be lived in

Beauty, shade, fragrance, cut flowers, and quiet places to sit. Wildness and care, held in balance so a space feels tended, not abandoned.

For the soil

Ground that gets richer

Deep-rooted plants, leaf litter left in place, and no needless chemicals — so soil life rebuilds, water soaks in, and the land stores more than it loses.

"The goal is simple: hand back a piece of ground healthier than I found it."
— The promise behind every project

How it works

Three steps, mostly painless.

Designed to respect your time: we do the thinking and planning before anyone schedules a visit, so the on-site day is focused and the install is the only big push.

01

Goals & site, gathered remotely No visit yet

We start with a conversation about what you want — habitat, color, low maintenance, food, privacy — and your budget. Then you send photos, a rough sketch or measurements, and notes on sun, water, and problem spots. I review it all and come back with a direction before we ever meet.

Intro callYou share photos & infoSun & site notesFirst direction
02

On-site consultation

Now we walk the space together. I check the soil, drainage, light, and existing plants worth keeping, confirm what will thrive where, and refine the plan into a concrete planting design with plant choices, quantities, and a clear cost.

Walk-throughSoil & drainage checkFinal planting planQuote
03

Sourcing & install

I source healthy, locally-grown native stock, prep the beds to set the plants up for life, and install — placing everything in layers so the planting can fill in and largely take care of itself. You get simple notes on watering it in and what to expect through the first year.
